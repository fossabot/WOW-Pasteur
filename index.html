Working Open Workshop 2017, Paris

Mozilla - Institut Pasteur

https://mzl.la/WOW-Paris

At this Working Open Workshop, Institut Pasteur and the Mozilla Science Lab, are co-organizing a two-day event around Open Science. On the first day, we will have an open conference with talks around Open Science and platforms for Open Collaboration On the second day, Mozilla Science Lab staff, Fellows and community will run a set of trainings to help prepare open science projects for project mentorship at the Institut Pasteur. This page will serve as a resource for all sessions materials.

Day 2: Working Open Workshop

Each session has a ten-minute presentation. Slides for these presentations are linked below. Exercises can be completed during work sessions.

Morning

  • Open Canvas - create a coherent strategy, including channels for contribution. - Exercise
  • Roadmapping - plan your project schedule and priorities. - Exercise
  • README - describe and dejargonize your project. - Exercise
  • Contributor Guidelines + Code of Conduct - facilitate contribution. - Ex.-Guidelines Ex.-CoC

Lunch

  • BOF - Software - talk about open software.
  • BOF - Design - talk about open design.
  • BOF - Funding - talk about open funding.
  • BOF - Global Sprint - talk about the Global Sprint.

Afternoon

  • Personas and Pathways - plan for your project users and their participation. - Exercise
  • Community Building Panel - develop plans for a variety of communities.
  • Open Data and Data Reuse Plans - manage data in your research and share it appropriately. - Exercise
  • Event Planning - plan community events and meetings that support and build your project.
  • Assessment and Metrics - collect data and evaluate projects.
  • Open Hardware and Tools - integrate physical components into projects.

Glossary

OLC

Open Leadership Cohort is a group of participants who have a shared experience and goal - to help further open practice in their work and community. It's what the WOW participants will be inducted into, you can read more about it on this blog.

Fellows

The Mozilla Science Lab has a group of awesome fellows in our annual fellowship program, the 2016 fellows are present at WOW, and will be co-running sessions throughout the event.

DIY science network

Lucy Patterson
Building a network of grassroots community-led, often volunteer-run citizen science/DIY science projects. The idea is to map and network our diverse communities, support each other through collaboration, sharing of tips, experience and information about funding opportunities, to help build a bridge towards funding organisations and policy-makers, and to support constructive collaboration with different partners.
